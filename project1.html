The true cost of healthcare for people with rare diseases has long been underestimated. Recent research shows that their medical expenses are three to five times higher than those of individuals without a rare disease.

These increased costs stem from frequent hospital visits, expensive treatments, specialized care, and prolonged diagnostic delays. Beyond direct medical expenses, rare disease patients and their families face significant indirect costs, including lost income, caregiver burdens, and out-of-pocket expenses not covered by insurance.

As awareness grows, experts stress the urgent need for better policies and financial support to ease the economic strain on affected families.
